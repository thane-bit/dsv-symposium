A conversation of national importance

As strategic advisor to DSIT’s TechFirst programme, Deep Science Ventures is supporting a conversation on the future of the scientific workforce.

Deep Science Ventures is convening an invitation-only forum of C-suite funders, deeptech founders, leading universities, and policymakers to move toward a concrete redesign of PhD and PG education, embedding AI co-scientists, incentivising venture creation, and bringing capital into training so that cohorts of fundable, globally competitive companies grow institutional revenues.

A reception hosted by Renaissance Philanthropy will consider key themes for a new philanthropic fund dedicated to accelerating UK prosperity. Conversations will proceed under Chatham House rules and summaries will be fed into the Future Skills Agenda.

Why now? The economic engine of invention

UK Science is at a crossroads. To our right, more universities face insolvency. International student fees have dwindled, austerity cuts are expected. To our left, automation. £2bn has been committed to sovereign AI, +1,300 Anthropic and OpenAI employees joining the Knowledge Quarter. AI scientists are already hypothesising.

What will be the new R&D paradigm fuelled by science automation and delivering economic growth? How can we prepare for it now?

If the UK is to remain a scientific superpower, the scientific PhD and postgraduate (PG) must evolve: who we train, and how we train. We must reimagine postgraduate science education so that invention is a first-class discipline, and one that funds more R&D on campus.

In the 1950s, the National Defence Education Act (NDEA) and the first Advanced Research Projects Agency (ARPA) launched PhD programmes which created the fields of electrical engineering, chemical engineering and computer science. $Trillion industries followed: Clean Energy, Pharma, AI. These PhDs invented far away from the beaten path. By learning from the past we can create the world’s next pathbreaking R&D ecosystem in the UK.

Funding the future of scientific training: scaling UK venture science

Deep Science Ventures has created a UK-grown national Venture Science Doctorate Programme that is already integrated into traditional PG and PhD pathways across leading UK, European and global HEIs.

Our model is best-in-class: based on a decade of building and funding venture science, creating +58 portfolio companies, £0.7bn across Computation, Agri, Climate and Pharma, including Mission Zero.

Invention is automatable. Venture Science is a repeatable curriculum and agentic workflow for problem selection, hypothesis-driven invention, and venture formation.

Incentives shape outcomes. Align PG training, IP policy, and funding so entrepreneurial scientists can form ventures during study without career penalty, and stay here.

Capital belongs on campus. Pair the Venture Science Doctorate with stage-gated, embedded capital and founder support within HEIs to turn talent density into venture density, and drive cultural change within our research labs.

Our goal is to scale: establishing a nation-wide, public–private capital model tied to invention milestones.
